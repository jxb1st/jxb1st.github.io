SA

Streaming Visual Assistant Benchmark

Situated question + answer benchmark for a smart-glasses AI assistant. 11 task types in 3 families: Proactive monitoring (event detection with repeating or first-occurrence trigger, interval tracking, threshold alerts), Reactive Q&A (localization with both past-placement & forward search, recall including temporal "when?", diagnosis & guidance, verification, and cross-time comparison), and Interaction dynamics (concurrent tracking, instruction update, causal chains). Gemini 2.5 Pro first role-plays the person to write the questions they would ask, then role-plays the AI assistant to emit the spoken responses — `(response_time, response_text)` lists for proactive instructions, single answers for reactive questions, with an honest can't-answer escape hatch.

11 task types 6 videos v4 questions v4a answers
video proactive + reactive Gemini 2.5 Pro

PM

Persona MVP — Proactive Interventions on EgoSchema

Persona-driven proactive-intervention generator on EgoSchema procedural clips. Gemini 2.5 Pro role-plays 8 explicit user personas (impatient novice, distracted, misreader, wrong-tool, careful-slow, overconfident, temperature-blind, expert-clean control); for each (clip, step, eligible persona) it watches the video and writes the deviation that persona would naturally make at that moment, together with a `(onset_ts, deviation_description, proactive_query, gold_guidance)` tuple that can be serialized into SPOT-Bench or Ego-MC-Bench training rows. The v2 prompt adds explicit contrast across peers to widen per-step deviation diversity. The wearer in the real video did NOT actually make this error — visual realism is out of scope for this MVP and is the next milestone on CaptainCook4D / HoloAssist.

15 clips 8 personas 144 items v2 contrast prompt
video persona-driven Gemini 2.5 Pro

EC

Event Clips — Gemini Segmentation

Per-event sub-clips of the EgoSchema and Action100M candidates, segmented by Gemini 2.5 Flash. For each 3-minute source video, Gemini emits a list of coherent sub-events (e.g. wash → chop → fry); ffmpeg cuts each event into its own mp4. The viewer shows the source video alongside an interactive event timeline and a grid of playable sub-clips.

175 source videos 1,514 event clips 2 datasets
